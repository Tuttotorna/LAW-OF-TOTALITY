# Claim Boundary

## Hard boundary

The Law of Totality should not be presented as already proven universal science.

Current status:

```text
formal-operational hypothesis with strong cross-domain fit
```

Not yet:

```text
externally validated universal law
```

## What may be claimed

Defensible:

> The Law of Totality proposes a unified structural criterion for error:
> a local closure becomes structurally invalid when used beyond its valid scope while excluding a critical dependency.

Defensible:

> The framework connects philosophical non-closure with operational audit practice.

Defensible:

> It may serve as a prevention protocol for AI hallucination, model misuse, premature closure, and invalid generalization.

Defensible:

> It reframes hallucination as a structural-scope failure, not only as a false sentence.

## What should not be claimed

Not defensible yet:

> This proves nobody in the world ever thought anything similar.

Not defensible yet:

> This proves all existing theories are wrong.

Not defensible yet:

> This automatically solves every problem.

Not defensible yet:

> This is validated because it is published on GitHub, Zenodo, or has a DOI.

Not defensible yet:

> No counterexample exists in the absolute sense.

## Correct public sentence

```text
Many disciplines already recognize fragments of this problem:
model risk, fallibilism, open systems, closed-world assumptions,
distribution shift, AI hallucination, and premature closure.

The Law of Totality attempts to unify those fragments into one operational formula:
structural error occurs when a local closure is used beyond its valid scope
while excluding a critical dependency.
```

## Minimal external entry point

The first public door should not be Ω.

The first public door should be:

```text
Correct is not stable.
A statement, model, or decision may be locally correct
and still structurally invalid when used outside its valid scope.
```

Then the formula follows.
