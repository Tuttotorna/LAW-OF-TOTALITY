# Example Audit: Business or Economic Decision

## Case

A business forecast is built on historical data.

The spreadsheet is internally coherent.

The projection appears rational.

However, the model excludes dependencies that are critical for the actual investment or strategic decision.

Examples:

- regulation;
- liquidity;
- supply-chain fragility;
- human behavior;
- geopolitical risk;
- energy cost;
- legal exposure;
- timing;
- market regime change;
- financing conditions;
- operational bottlenecks.

---

## Audit table

| Field | Answer |
|---|---|
| Object | Business forecast or investment decision |
| Framework | Spreadsheet, statistical projection, historical model, internal forecast |
| Actual use | Real allocation of money, labor, inventory, infrastructure or strategic commitment |
| Valid scope | Scenario assumptions and historical continuity represented in the model |
| Local closure present? | yes, if the forecast is treated as sufficient |
| Critical dependencies for this use | regulation, liquidity, supply chain, timing, legal risk, market behavior, operational constraints |
| Excluded critical dependencies | any dependency required by the actual decision but missing from the model |
| Use beyond valid scope? | yes, if forecast is used as sufficient basis for action beyond modeled assumptions |
| Structural error present? | yes |
| Explanation | The forecast may be locally coherent, but the decision becomes structurally invalid if it relies on excluded dependencies that are critical in reality. |

---

## Structural diagnosis

The spreadsheet can be correct.

The decision can still be structurally wrong.

The error is not arithmetic.

The error is false sufficiency.

The structural error is:

> model coherence mistaken for decision validity.

---

## Corrective rule

Before action, the audit must ask:

1. What assumptions does the model require?
2. What dependencies does the decision require?
3. Which dependencies are not represented?
4. Which missing dependencies can invalidate the action?
5. Is the forecast being treated as sufficient?

---

## Minimal conclusion

Business decision failure can be structurally interpreted as:

> internally coherent projection + real decision beyond projection scope + excluded critical dependency.
