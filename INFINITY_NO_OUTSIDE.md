## 2. Why Ω Does Not Depend

A proper manifestation is something that manifests inside Ω and is not Ω itself.

~~~text
ProperΩ(x) ⇔ ManifestΩ(x) ∧ x ≠ Ω
~~~

The law applies only to proper manifestations:

~~~text
∀x ∈ 𝔻 :
ProperΩ(x) ⇒ ∃d [DetCondΩ(d,x)]
~~~

Expanded:

~~~text
∀x ∈ 𝔻 :
[ManifestΩ(x) ∧ x ≠ Ω]
⇒
∃d [DetCondΩ(d,x)]
~~~

For Ω:

~~~text
x = Ω
~~~

Therefore the antecedent does not apply.

## 7. Philosophical Position

This does not prove the mathematical existence of infinity as a new object.

It formalizes the role of infinity inside the law.

The role is:

~~~text

the no-outside case is the only non-dependent case
every proper manifestation is dependent because it is not the no-outside case
~~~

Therefore the law is not broken by Ω.

Ω is the condition that makes the law type-correct.
