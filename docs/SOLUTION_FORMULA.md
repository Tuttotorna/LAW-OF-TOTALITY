# Solution Formula

## Solution as reintegration into totality

The formula of solution in the Law of Totality is:

S_Ω(x) = lim_{A→Ω} F_A*(x)

where:

F_A*(x) = arg min_{F_A} Δ(F_A(x), Ω_A(x))

Equivalent compact form:

S_Ω(x) = lim_{A→Ω} arg min_{F_A} Δ(F_A(x), Ω_A(x))

## Meaning

The solution is not simply a correct answer.

The solution is the progressive correction of the fragment as the accessible total field expands.

At each approximation level A, the local fragment F_A(x) must be corrected into F_A*(x), the form that minimizes the distance from Ω_A(x).

## Simple form

Solution = fragment reintegrated into totality.

## Operational meaning

A problem is not truly solved by optimizing a local fragment alone.

A problem is solved when the fragment is returned to the widest accessible field from which it depends, and its distance from that field is minimized.

## Perfect solution

A perfect solution would require:

Δ(F_A*(x), Ω_A(x)) = 0

at the limit as:

A→Ω

A finite intelligence may not possess the totality absolutely.

But it can reduce error by widening the field and refusing to treat any fragment as autonomous or total.
