# Error Formula

## Error as distance from totality

The formula of error in the Law of Totality is:

E_Ω(x) = lim_{A→Ω} Δ(F_A(x), Ω_A(x))

This formula states that error is not merely an incorrect output.

Error is the remaining distance between a local fragment and the total structural field from which that fragment depends.

## Terms

- x: any object, event, decision, answer, model, action, or phenomenon.
- F_A(x): the local fragment, representation, or reduction used at approximation level A.
- Ω_A(x): the accessible or observable total structural field at approximation level A.
- Ω(x): the absolute total structural field.
- Δ: distance, incompatibility, or incongruence.
- A→Ω: progressive expansion of the accessible field toward totality.

## Meaning

A finite intelligence never begins with complete access to Ω.

It operates through Ω_A.

The error decreases when the accessible field expands and the fragment becomes more compatible with that field.

The error increases when the fragment is isolated, absolutized, or mistaken for the whole.

## Simple form

Error = fragment separated from totality.

## Mother error

The mother error is not fragmentation itself.

Fragmentation is necessary for finite operation.

The mother error begins when a fragment forgets that it is a fragment.
