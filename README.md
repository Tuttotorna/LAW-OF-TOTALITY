## The Law of Totality

No fragment is independent from the totality.

Every structural error begins when a local fragment is treated as autonomous, sufficient, or total.

LAW-OF-TOTALITY is the foundational repository for the Law of Totality: a universal structural law describing error as the distance between a local fragment and the total structural field from which that fragment depends.

This repository is not limited to mathematics, artificial intelligence, economics, ethics, politics, or science.

It states a more general principle:

nothing can be understood correctly if it is treated as an independent fragment.

## Core formulas

### Formula of error

E_Ω(x) = lim_{A→Ω} Δ(F_A(x), Ω_A(x))

### Formula of solution

S_Ω(x) = lim_{A→Ω} F_A*(x)

where:

F_A*(x) = arg min_{F_A} Δ(F_A(x), Ω_A(x))

Equivalent compact form:

S_Ω(x) = lim_{A→Ω} arg min_{F_A} Δ(F_A(x), Ω_A(x))

## Meaning

A finite mind or artificial intelligence cannot immediately possess the absolute totality Ω.

It can only operate through an accessible or observable total field Ω_A at approximation level A.

The Law of Totality does not claim omniscience.

It states that every local fragment F_A(x) must be tested, corrected, and expanded against wider and wider fields of dependence.

Error is the remaining distance between the local fragment and the accessible total field.

Solution is the progressive correction of the fragment as the accessible field expands toward the totality.

## Central equivalence

Error = fragment separated from totality.

Solution = fragment reintegrated into totality.
