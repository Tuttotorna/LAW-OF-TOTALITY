# Review Questions — v0.9.0

## Primary Question

Is the Structural Error Formula a useful portable audit predicate, or only a restatement of existing ideas?

## Core Questions

1. Is the distinction between local correctness and valid use technically useful?

2. Does the object-framework-use structure add clarity?

3. Is actual use U the correct pivot point?

4. Is excluded critical dependency the correct failure condition?

5. Are ValidScope(F,x) and CriticalDepΩ(d,x,U) definable enough for operational use?

6. Can the formula classify negative controls correctly?

7. Does the Error Atlas demonstrate range or merely produce analogies?

8. Which existing field already handles this best?

9. What does the Law of Totality add, if anything?

10. What would falsify or weaken the framework?

## Domain-Specific Questions

### AI Safety / AI Governance

Can the formula help distinguish:

    AI output as draft
    AI output as orientation
    AI output as verified fact
    AI output as professional advice
    AI output as high-stakes decision input

### Model Risk

Does the formula generalize model-risk failure from quantitative models to arbitrary frameworks?

### Systems Safety

Is the formula too weak compared to STPA/STAMP, or useful outside safety engineering?

### Philosophy / Epistemology

Is the framework only map-territory with extra notation, or does actual use U create a meaningful difference?

### Formal Methods / Logic

Is the notation meaningful enough, or only suggestive?

## Counterexample Request

Please provide one example where:

    all formula components are identified;
    the formula gives the wrong verdict;
    or the components cannot be identified without arbitrariness.
